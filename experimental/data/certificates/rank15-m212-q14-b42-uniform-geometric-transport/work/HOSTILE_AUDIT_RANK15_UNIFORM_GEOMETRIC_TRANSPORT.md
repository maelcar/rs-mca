## Repaired `D=68` bridge

The worker draft asserted, without proof, that every non-side small point lies
in all three low pencils. The executable checked only the arithmetic after
that assertion. Publication was held until the following lemma was added.

With three no-heavy lines, an off-side multiplicity-`k` point uses at least
`k-3` no-heavy lines, while a point on a heavy side uses at least `k-2`.
Distinct points cannot use the same pair of no-heavy lines.

- In the `4^26,5^3` profile, the three fivefold points consume the three
  no-heavy pairs separately and exhaust them.
- In the `4^29,6` profile, the sixfold point lies on all three no-heavy lines
  and consumes all three pairs.

Any remaining fourfold point on a side or missing a low-pencil direction
would need an already consumed pair. Thus all small points have the claimed
three-pencil concurrency. The verifier now replays the finite pair-allocation
lemma before applying the correlation and Kneser checks.

With that repair, the correlation floor 131 and the Kneser contradiction are
valid.
