## 2. The theorem (verbatim) and its exact scope

`thm:capf-dim2` (projective-plane pair bound, raw L6719):

> Under the hypotheses of \cref{lem:capf-concurrency}, assume $j\ge2$
> and $\dim\PP(W)=2$.  Then
> $|\PP(W)\cap\Dloc_j(H)| \le \binom{|H|}{2}/(j-1)$,
> and if the evaluation lines $E_h$ are pairwise distinct, the sharper
> bound with denominator $\binom j2$ holds.

Hypotheses, exactly: `W <= K[X]_{<=j}` nonzero with no `h in H` a
common root of all of `W` (gcd-triviality, `lem:capf-concurrency` raw
L6707 — exactly what the paid common-GCD first-match strip
`lem:capf-gcd` raw L6672 guarantees), `j >= 2`, `dim P(W) = 2`
exactly.  No balanced-core and no `mu_n` hypothesis; `H = D` works with
`|H| = n = 2^21`, `j = omega`.  Derivation of `C(n,2)/(j-1)`,
summarized: the `E_h` are lines in the plane `P(W)`; no coincidence
class has multiplicity `>= j` (such a class would be a 2-dimensional
projective space inside the 0-dimensional span of one locator — proved
unconditionally, and verified on every exhibited plane below); each
locator point carries class multiplicities summing to `j`, each
`<= j-1`, hence at least `min_m m(j-m) = j-1` cross pairs `{h,h'}` with
`E_h != E_{h'}`; each cross pair is charged to the unique intersection
point `E_h cap E_{h'}`, at most once; at most `C(|H|,2)` pairs exist.
The count is an integer, so the floor of the rational bound is booked.

**PER-CHART SCOPE (NON-CLAIM).**  The theorem bounds LOCATOR POINTS PER
CHART (one fixed `W`).  No chart-multiplicity-per-line bound is in
hand — this is exactly the objection `prop:bc-not-q` (gf L2120) raises
("a line-by-line decomposition without a bound on the number of lines
gives no row budget").  Booking the cell as a single row consumer
therefore inherits budget-fit P2's accepted bookkeeping, and every
ledger sentence below is scoped accordingly.  Converting that
bookkeeping into a per-row chart-count theorem is open and NOT claimed.
