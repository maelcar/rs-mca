## S2 -- WHY NO CHANGE IS FORCED (the necessary-not-sufficient argument)

The temptation is to read #668's unconditional `rho* <= log(3/2)` as discharging
the tex's image-scale hard input. It does not, for three independent reasons, each
recorded verbatim in the arc:

1. **Different object (scope).** #668 caps the degree-2 moment-curve *subset-sum
   packing constant* on a single block. The tex's `"image-scale MI and MA or a
   direct Sidon payment"` (Row 1) is the payment for the *primitive residual across
   all first-match leaves*. #668's own note states it "does not supply the
   source-specific first-match atlas, block embedding, profile add-back, or slope
   charge needed to close A4 ... proves no deployed adjacent-row inequality and
   neither Grand MCA nor Grand List." The atom closure is **necessary but not
   sufficient** for the printed input.

2. **Not printed (no consumer).** Row A: the packing constant, `(ILO-moment)`,
   `omega(eta)`, and the census never entered the manuscript. There is no printed
   sub-clause to mark discharged, and no printed number to correct.

3. **Our own consumer note declines the edit.** #673's Nonclaims, verbatim: *"No
   span-face content. No TeX edit."* The reconciliation that established the
   both-ends-unconditional bracket explicitly did not identify a manuscript change.

**Why not TIGHTENED?** The task primes: is the open phrasing the sharpest form, or
should it adopt #663/#682's wall statement? #663/#682's sharpest wall is *"one
Bohr->GAP step on the eps*b complement / the exponential-regime inverse-LO in the
packing corridor `delta >= 1/3`"* -- but #673 records that this GAP-form wall is
**"no longer load-bearing for any consumer in this program"** (since #668 bounds
`L1` directly by compression, bypassing GAP structure). Importing it into the tex's
image-scale input would narrow a broad, genuinely open manuscript hypothesis to a
solved-around note sub-question -- an **overclaim in disguise**. The tex phrasing is
therefore already the sharpest *honest* form, and TIGHTENED does not apply.

**Quantifier honesty.** Unconditional **today at base `36de5bf`**: nothing new on
the printed image face (the arc lives in the notes). Unconditional **on the note
face today**: `rho* <= log(3/2)` requires #668, which is *not integrated at base*
-- at base the notes carry only the conditional cap under `(ILO-moment)`. The
both-ends bracket `[0.158411, 0.405465]` **activates upon integration of #668**
(and its reconciliation #673). No tex row inherits this dependency because no tex
row prints the object.
