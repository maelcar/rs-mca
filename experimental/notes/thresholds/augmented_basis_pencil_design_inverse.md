## 1. Setup

Use the weighted-RS chart of the paving compiler.  Thus `U` has `N` distinct
coordinates,

```text
H : F^U -> F^R,          K=ker H,          dim K=kappa,
N=R+kappa,
```

and `G` is an `N x kappa` generator matrix of the MDS code `K`.  Choose lifts

```text
H b_0=y_0,        H b_1=y_1,
```

and put

```text
A=[b_0 b_1 G].
```

For a `(kappa+1)`-set `I subset U`, call `I` an augmented basis when
`rank(A_I)=kappa+1`, and write

```text
beta=beta_(kappa+1)(A)
    =#{I subset U: |I|=kappa+1, rank(A_I)=kappa+1}.       (2)
```

Let `P` be any retained pair family satisfying the source hypotheses.  For
`p=(gamma,c) in P` put

```text
Z_p={x:c_x=0},
b_p=#{I subset Z_p: |I|=kappa+1, I is an augmented basis}. (3)
```

The source theorem proves `rank(A_(Z_p))=kappa+1` and that no augmented basis
is contained in the zero sets of two distinct retained pairs.

## 2. Basis deficiency is exactly common-flat incidence

For `S subset U` let

```text
V_S=span_F{h_x:x in S}.
```

### Theorem 1 (common-flat complement identity)

One has the exact identity

```text
binom(N,kappa+1)-beta
 =#{S subset U: |S|=R-1 and y_0,y_1 in V_S}.             (4)
```

#### Proof

Complementation identifies an `(R-1)`-set `S` with a
`(kappa+1)`-set `I=U\S`.  Every `kappa` rows of `G` are independent, hence
`rank(G_I)=kappa`.

The condition `y_i in V_S` says that some word `e_i` supported on `S` has
`H e_i=y_i`.  Equivalently, `b_i-e_i in K`, so

```text
(b_i)_I in col(G_I).                                    (5)
```

Thus `y_0,y_1 in V_S` if and only if both hosted columns of `A_I` lie in the
`kappa`-dimensional column space of `G_I`.  This is equivalent to
`rank(A_I)=kappa`, rather than `kappa+1`.  Counting complementary sets proves
(4).  `square`

If a fixed `s`-set `S_0` with `s<=R-1` already spans both syndromes, then every
`(R-1)`-superset of `S_0` is counted in (4), so

```text
binom(N,kappa+1)-beta >= binom(N-s,R-1-s).               (6)
```

This is the correct direction of a common-support signal: basis
*deficiency*, not basis heaviness.

### Corollary 2 (deep-hole heaviness is automatic)

Let

```text
d=min{wt(v):H v=y_1}.
```

If `d=R`, then `K+<b_1>` is an `[N,kappa+1,R]` MDS code generated by
`[b_1 G]`.  Every `kappa+1` rows of that column submatrix are independent.
They remain independent in `A`, proving (1).

The conclusion is independent of `b_0`.  In particular, `A` may have global
rank `kappa+1` when `y_0` and `y_1` are proportional, or rank `kappa+2` when
they are independent, while `beta` has the same maximal value.

## 3. Exact core-pencil disintegration

Fix a `kappa`-set `J subset U`.  Since `G_J` is invertible, for `i=0,1` there
is a unique lift in the hosted coset that vanishes on `J`:

```text
u_(i,J)=b_i-G G_J^(-1)(b_i)_J.                          (7)
```

Define its common-zero core and capacity by

```text
C_J=Z(u_(0,J)) intersect Z(u_(1,J)),
g_J=|C_J|,                 c_J=N-g_J.                   (8)
```

The set `J` is contained in `C_J`.

### Lemma 3 (one-coordinate basis test)

For every `x notin J`,

```text
J union {x} is an augmented basis
    iff
x notin C_J.                                             (9)
```

Indeed, use the invertible `G_J` block to eliminate the first two columns on
the rows `J`.  The remaining row raises the rank from `kappa` to
`kappa+1` exactly when
`(u_(0,J)(x),u_(1,J)(x))!=(0,0)`.

Counting every augmented basis once for each of its `kappa+1` subcores gives

```text
sum_(|J|=kappa) (N-g_J)=(kappa+1) beta.                 (10)
```

Now put

```text
P_J={p in P:J subset Z_p}.
```

If `p=(gamma,c) in P_J`, uniqueness on `J` gives the literal pencil equation

```text
c=u_(0,J)+gamma u_(1,J).                                (11)
```

Two distinct members of `P_J` have distinct slopes: at a fixed slope,
vanishing on `J` uniquely determines the kernel coordinates.  Moreover their
moving zero sets

```text
Z_p\C_J,             p in P_J,
```

are pairwise disjoint.  If a coordinate outside `C_J` vanished at two
different slopes, subtracting the two equations in (11) would force first
`u_(1,J)(x)=0` and then `u_(0,J)(x)=0`, a contradiction.

Define the pencil load

```text
ell_J=sum_(p in P_J)(|Z_p|-g_J).                         (12)
```

Moving-zero disjointness gives the local capacity inequality

```text
ell_J<=N-g_J=c_J.                                       (13)
```

### Theorem 4 (exact basis-to-pencil ledger)

The local basis counts in (3) satisfy

```text
(kappa+1) sum_(p in P) b_p = sum_(|J|=kappa) ell_J.     (14)
```

Consequently the entire paving slack has the exact decomposition

```text
beta-sum_p b_p
 =1/(kappa+1) sum_(|J|=kappa) ((N-g_J)-ell_J).          (15)
```

#### Proof

Count triples `(p,J,x)` for which `p in P`, `J subset Z_p` has size
`kappa`, and `x in Z_p\C_J`.  By (9), `J union {x}` is an augmented basis
inside `Z_p`.  Every such basis has exactly `kappa+1` representations obtained
by deleting one of its coordinates, proving (14).  Subtract (14) from (10) to
obtain (15).  `square`

### Stability consequence

Suppose

```text
sum_p b_p >= (1-epsilon) beta.                           (16)
```

For `0<tau<=1`, call a core `tau`-bad when

```text
(N-g_J)-ell_J > tau(N-g_J).
```

Equations (10) and (15), followed by Markov's inequality with capacity
weights, give

```text
sum_(J tau-bad)(N-g_J)
 <= (epsilon/tau) sum_J(N-g_J).                         (17)
```

Thus near saturation forces almost all *capacity* onto nearly filled local
pencils.  It does not force the pencils to share a global core.

When `d=R`, Corollary 2 and (9) give the stronger pointwise identity

```text
g_J=kappa,             c_J=R                            (18)
```

for every `J`.  The deep-hole inverse is therefore a uniformly distributed
pencil-filling problem.

## 4. Equality is a Steiner system

Put `a=N-t`.  Under `d=R` every `(kappa+1)`-subset is an augmented basis, so

```text
b_p=binom(|Z_p|,kappa+1).
```

Assume the *unfloored* PB5 equality

```text
|P| binom(a,kappa+1)=binom(N,kappa+1).                  (19)
```

Because `|Z_p|>=a` and distinct pairs cannot own the same basis, equality
forces

```text
|Z_p|=a             for every p in P,
```

and every `(kappa+1)`-set of `U` lies in exactly one block `Z_p`.  Hence

```text
{Z_p:p in P} is a Steiner system S(kappa+1,a,N).         (20)
```

This conclusion requires equality in the rational bound, not merely equality
after flooring a nonintegral ratio.  Near equality gives an almost-Steiner
packing in the precise capacity sense of (17), but no classification of such
packings is asserted.

## 5. Positive-depth equality without a global core

Let `q=p^m` with prime `p>=3` and `m>=2`, take `U=F_q`, and let `K=<1>` be
the constant code.  Then

```text
N=q,       kappa=1,       R=q-1.
```

Choose

```text
b_1(x)=x,             b_0(x)=-x^p.                     (21)
```

Every word in `b_1+K` has the form `x+c` and exactly one zero, so its weight is
`q-1=R`.  Thus `d=R`.  Set

```text
a=p,       t=q-p,       h=a-kappa-1=p-2>0.              (22)
```

For every affine `F_p`-line `L=a_0+F_p v`, `v!=0`, its monic locator is

```text
Q_L(X)=(X-a_0)^p-v^(p-1)(X-a_0)
      =X^p-gamma X-z,
gamma=v^(p-1),         z=a_0^p-gamma a_0.               (23)
```

Therefore the retained word

```text
c_(gamma,z)(x)=-x^p+gamma x+z=-Q_L(x)
```

has zero set exactly `L` and weight `t`.  Conversely, a polynomial of this
form with at least `p` roots has exactly `p` roots forming an affine
`F_p`-line.  These are the complete radius-`t` pairs.

The family is transverse.  If `y_1=H b_1` lay in the parity-column span on the
error support, then some constant kernel translate of `b_1` would vanish on
the complementary line `L`.  But `x` is not constant on a line of size
`p>=3`.

The number of affine lines is

```text
|P|=q(q-1)/(p(p-1))
   =binom(q,2)/binom(p,2),                               (24)
```

so PB5 is attained exactly.  The zero sets form the affine-geometry Steiner
system

```text
S(2,p,q):
every pair of points lies on exactly one F_p-affine line. (25)
```

The functions `1,x,x^p` are linearly independent on `F_q`, so `A` has full
rank three.  The intersection of all affine lines is empty.  Thus the
saturating family has neither augmented-rank collapse nor a global common
core.  Its defining Frobenius map gives explicit finite-geometry structure,
but the full domain is `F_q` rather than a subset of a proper subfield.
Frobenius covariance alone therefore does not prove C5 ownership, and this
packet makes no primitive/nonprimitive first-match classification.
