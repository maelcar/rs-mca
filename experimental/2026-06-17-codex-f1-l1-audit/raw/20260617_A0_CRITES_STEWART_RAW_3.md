A secondary summary of CS25 states a theorem close to the repository import: small correlated-agreement error for `RS(D,k)` implies list decodability of `RS(D,k+1)`, with list bound
[
L=\left\lceil \frac{\epsilon q(q-n)}{q-n-k\epsilon q}\right\rceil,
]
and the simplification (L\le 2\epsilon q) under (\epsilon<(q-n)/(2kq)). That algebraically supports Paper D’s displayed `thm:A` at (\eta=1/2), but the summary is not an authoritative substitute for CS25/ABF26. ([HackMD][2])

The BCHKS fallback **was accessible** through the official ECCC PDF. Its Theorem 1.9 is an existential proximity-gap statement at (\gamma=\mathrm{LDR}_{\mathbb F_q,D,q}(\delta)+2/n), giving at least (q/(2n)) bad slopes and (\Delta([f,g],C^2)\ge\delta-1/n). That is not, by itself, the same statement as Paper D’s imported `thm:B`; the ABF26 restatement or a derivation is still needed.

## 3. Finite/asymptotic, field-size, constants, and promotion checks

**Finite vs asymptotic.** Paper D’s main cap is a finite statement: fixed (q<2^{256}), (k≤2^{40}), exact divisibility (N|n), exact binomial inequalities. It does not depend on the asymptotic locator or residue-line local-limit conjectures in `slackMCA_v3`. The only non-internal dependency is the imported CS conversion.

**`q≥n` and `q≥2n`.** Paper D correctly gets (n≤q-1) from `D⊂F^×`; this is enough for the weaker (2^{-86}) bound. The stronger (2^{-42}) tier really requires (q≥2n). The older Paper B cap also required (n≤q/2); Paper D’s main grand cap does not.

**Rate/radius constants.** The binomial constants in Paper D’s table are aligned with its proof:
[
N=1024,\ \ell_2=\rho N+2
]
for (\rho=1/2,1/4,1/8), and (N=2048,\ell_2=130) for (\rho=1/16). The gap is (2/N), hence (2^{-9}) or (2^{-10}). The arithmetic supporting (2^{-86}) uses (n≤16k≤2^{44}), hence (\frac1{2k(n+1)}>2^{-86}). This part checks internally.

**Radius range problem.** The repository import states CS for (\delta<\delta_{\min}(C)=1-\rho+1/n). The accessible secondary CS summary instead describes an integer error condition (f<n-k-1). If that is the real CS hypothesis, Paper D’s theorem statement over ([1-\rho-2/N,\delta_{\min})) is too broad, and even the no-loss `eca(C,δ)` lower bound near (1-\rho) needs a rounding argument. The safer correction is:
[
\delta\in[1-\rho-2/N,,1-\rho)
]
for Paper D’s `eca` claim, or prove failure at the lower endpoint and use monotonicity only for `emca`.

**List decoding to CA/MCA.** Paper D is not silently promoting list decoding to CA: it explicitly uses the CS conversion as the bridge. But that means the entire bridge is conditional. The CA-to-MCA step `eca≤emca` is proved internally for the no-loss definition at `tex/cs25_cap_v4.tex:118–123`; that part is fine. The BCHKS fallback is different: it is a two-radius/slacked CA statement, not the same no-loss CA/MCA cap.

**Interleaved lists.** Paper D’s interleaving lemma is a lower-bound transfer: if the base code has CA/MCA failure, the interleaved code also has failure. That is valid for obstruction claims. It is not a protocol soundness upper bound on (|\Lambda(\mathrm{Int}(C,\mu),\delta)|/q), and Paper C correctly treats that as a separate ledger item.

## 4. Final verdict

**AUDIT.**

No counterexample was found in Paper D’s internal algebra. The cap proof is a shallow and plausible contrapositive composition:

1. locator fibers give a large list in (C^+=RS[F,D,k+1]);
2. CS says small CA error would force all such lists to be small;
3. contradiction gives (\eca) failure;
4. internal `eca≤emca` gives MCA failure.

But the exact CS source theorem was not available, and the accessible secondary statement raises unresolved interface questions: radius/off-by-one, finite-field generality, exact `C+`, and normalization. Therefore Paper D’s universal field-size cap should remain **CONDITIONAL/AUDIT**, not **PROVED**.

## 5. Top five exact fixes/citations needed

1. **Attach or cite the exact CS25 Theorem 2 text.** The repository needs a frozen statement with `C=RS[F,D,k]`, `C+=RS[F,D,k+1]`, the exact field class, radius range, CA definition, and list-size formula. A secondary summary is not enough.

2. **Resolve the radius/off-by-one issue.** If CS is stated in terms of an integer error bound (f), add a lemma translating real (\delta) to (f=\lfloor \delta n\rfloor). Unless CS really covers (\delta<\delta_{\min}(C)), change `tex/cs25_cap_v4.tex:224–230` to sub-capacity radii only.

3. **Keep all cap statements explicitly conditional.** The abstract and theorem prose in Paper D should say “conditional on CS25/ABF26” until the import is verified. The caveat at `tex/cs25_cap_v4.tex:425–426` is correct but too late to prevent overcitation.

4. **Demote BCHKS fallback to a separate slacked statement.** The official BCHKS theorem accessible here is an LDR/proximity-gap theorem, not Paper D’s displayed implication. Cite ABF26 Theorem 5.2 or provide the derivation from BCHKS Theorem 1.9, including strict inequalities and the (\delta+2/n) shift.

5. **Make the field ledger explicit in Paper D’s theorem environment.** State `q=|F|=q_line`; state `B` is the field of definition for locator coefficients, ideally minimal for `q_gen`; state `q_chal` is not being used unless a protocol reduction identifies it with `q_line`. This prevents extension-field double-crediting.
