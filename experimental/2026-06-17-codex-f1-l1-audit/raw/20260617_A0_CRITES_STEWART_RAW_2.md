## 3. Specific hostile checks

### Finite vs. asymptotic

Paper D’s arithmetic is finite: `q<2^256`, `k≤2^40`, explicit `N=1024` or `2048`, and explicit binomial lower bounds. The BCHKS fallback theorem I could access is also a finite theorem with explicit `q/(2n)` bad slopes. ([Mathematics at University of Toronto][2])

The unresolved issue is not asymptotic looseness; it is source-theorem exactness: CS25/ABF26 must be fetched to verify finite hypotheses, radii, field scope, and strict/weak inequalities.

### `q≥n` and `q≥2n`

`q≥n+1` is automatic in Paper D because `D⊂F×` has order `n`, so `n≤q−1`. Paper D correctly removes the old `q≥2n` assumption by using the worst case `q=n+1`, giving
`(1/(2k))(1−n/q) ≥ 1/(2k(n+1)) > 2^-86` under `k≤2^40`, `ρ≥1/16`.

`q≥2n` is only needed for the stronger `≥2^-42` bound. Paper B’s older cap still needs `n≤q/2`.

### Rate, radius, and list-size constants

The rate constants in Paper D are arithmetically consistent:

|   Rate |    `N` | `ρN+2` | Gap `2/N` |      Claimed cap |
| -----: | -----: | -----: | --------: | ---------------: |
|  `1/2` | `1024` |  `514` |    `2^-9` |    `δ*≤1/2−2^-9` |
|  `1/4` | `1024` |  `258` |    `2^-9` |    `δ*≤3/4−2^-9` |
|  `1/8` | `1024` |  `130` |    `2^-9` |    `δ*≤7/8−2^-9` |
| `1/16` | `2048` |  `130` |   `2^-10` | `δ*≤15/16−2^-10` |

The CS-path list-size contradiction is also internally consistent: at `η=1/2`, the imported theorem would give `Lst(C+,δ)≤ceil(2q·eca)`, and the assumed `eca≤(1/(2k))(1−n/q)` gives an upper bound `<q/k+1`, contradicting the fiber lower bound `≥q/k+1`.

The weak point is the **upper radius**. If CS25 only applies to integer radii `f<n−k−1`, then Paper D cannot directly claim `eca(C,δ)>...` for every `δ<1−ρ`. The cap on `δ*` can still be repaired by proving the lower bound at `δ0=1−ρ−2/N` and using support-wise MCA monotonicity, but the displayed `eca` statement would need narrowing.

### List decoding vs. CA/MCA

Paper D does **not** silently promote list decoding to MCA in the main proof. It explicitly uses:

1. locator fibers to produce a large list;
2. CS import to convert “large list” into “CA error is not small”;
3. `eca(C,δ)≤emca(C,δ)` to transfer CA to support-wise MCA.

The hidden danger is elsewhere: the actual locator fiber line is `B`-rational, and over a proper extension `F/B` such base-rational lines have bad-slope density at most `|B|/|F|`. Paper D correctly notes that the certifying extension-field CA/MCA lines are nonconstructive and genuinely `F`-valued. Do not cite the explicit locator line itself as the MCA witness over the extension.

   Add a sentence: “The imported CS/ABF theorem applies to RS codes over arbitrary finite fields `F` and samples slopes uniformly from that same `F`.” If the source does not say this, the extension-field cap must be downgraded.

4. **Fix the BCHKS fallback strictness.**
   From the accessible theorem, the safe contrapositive is triggered by list size `>q`, not merely `≥q`. Change `binom(N,ρN+1) ≥ |B|q` to a strict `>` or `≥ |B|(q+1)`, unless ABF26 Theorem 5.2 really proves Paper D’s displayed `Lst(C,δ)<|F|`.

   State explicitly: `q_gen=|B|`, `q_line=|F|`, `q_chal` only after protocol reduction, `B` pays the locator entropy, `F` pays the CA/MCA slope density. This prevents the extension field from being double-counted as generated-field entropy or protocol soundness without a transfer theorem.
