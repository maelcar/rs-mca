\section{The frontier programme: terminal-scale certificates and the closing conjecture}\label{sec:v13f-frontier}

The two-sided sandwich of \cref{thm:sandwich2} leaves, at the deployed rate-$\tfrac12$ rows, the open band between the imported safe edge $\tfrac14$ and the unsafe edge of \cref{rem:exact-frontier}.  This section does three things.  First, it extends the exact $(c,m)$ optimization of \cref{rem:exact-frontier} to \emph{every} dyadic scale down to the terminal scale $c=2$, which strictly lowers all four deployed unsafe edges and pins the graded-prefix route to its exact integer frontier, with adjacent pass/fail certificates at every scale.  Second, it proves two closure lemmas showing that the known cost-free witness mechanisms --- sub-period planting and subgroup-invariant subset selection --- cannot extend the route past this frontier.  Third, it states the resulting \emph{frontier conjecture} for the exact challenge threshold, records its provable floors and its precise reduction to two already-named inputs, so that the final theorem has a definite shape: an adjacent staircase pair at the printed agreement.

\subsection{Terminal-scale exact certificates}

Throughout, $n=2^{21}$, $k=2^{20}$, and the rows, fields, targets, and conversion thresholds are those of \cref{cor:widened-deployed} and \cref{rem:exact-frontier}: the test at scale $c$, quotient order $N=n/c$, is
\[
\binom Nm>|\B|^{\,w}\Bigl(\frac qk+1\Bigr)
\quad\text{\textup(MCA, $K=k+1$\textup)},
\qquad
\binom Nm\cdot 2^{\lambda}>|\B|^{\,w}q
\quad\text{\textup(list, $K=k$\textup)},
\]
with $w=m-\ceil{K/c}$ and $\lambda=128$ \textup(resp.\ $\lambda=100$ for the circle list target\textup); the certified unsafe range is then $\delta\in[1-mc/n,\,1-\rho)$ as in \cref{cor:widened-deployed}.

Admissibility of $c=2$. The conversions to $\emca>2^{-22}$ \textup(resp.\ the circle thresholds\textup) and to the grand list challenge are then verbatim those of \cref{cor:widened-deployed}, with \cref{fact:chain} and \cref{lem:mca-monotone} extending the MCA bound to the displayed closed ranges.

Exactness and adjacency.  For each dyadic scale $c$ and each row, the largest admissible $m$ was located and then certified by \emph{exact integer} evaluation of the displayed inequality at $m$ \textup(pass\textup) and $m+1$ \textup(fail\textup); binomials were computed exactly and compared as integers against $|\B|^wq$-type products, with no logarithmic shortcut in any certified comparison.  The per-scale optima are strictly increasing in $N=n/c$ throughout the scanned lattice

Since \cref{prop:graded-prefix-floor} requires $\deg\varphi=c\ge2$ and every scale divides $n=2^{21}$, the scale $c=2$ is terminal, and the printed certificates are the exact optimum of the route at the deployed blocklength.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Closure of the route under the free mechanisms}

Two mechanisms produce list mass without pigeonhole cost: planting fewer than $c$ extra points inside one fiber \textup(\cref{thm:v13-planted}\textup), and restricting the subset family to a subgroup-invariant class so that prefix slots vanish identically.  Neither extends the route past \cref{prop:v13f-terminal}.

\begin{lemma}[scale absorption of invariant selection]\label{lem:v13f-scale-absorption}
Let $Q=\mu_N\subseteq\B$ \textup(or a coset thereof after normalization\textup), let $d\mid N$, and let $A\subseteq Q$ be a union of complete $\mu_d$-cosets, $|A|=m$.  Then
\[
        \prod_{b\in A}(Y-b)=g\bigl(Y^d\bigr)
        \qquad\text{with}\qquad
        g(T)=\prod_{\bar b\in A^d}(T-\bar b),\ \ |A^d|=m/d ,
\]
where $A^d$ is the image of $A$ under the $d$-power map.  In particular $e_j(A)=0$ whenever $d\nmid j$, and the graded prefix floor evaluated on the $\mu_d$-invariant stratum at scale $c$ coincides with the floor at the composite scale $cd$: invariant selection pins prefix slots for free but reduces the subset entropy by exactly the same factor, so the per-scale optimum of \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} already dominates it.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Each $\mu_d$-coset $b\mu_d$ contributes $\prod_{u\in\mu_d}(Y-bu)=Y^d-b^d$, so the locator of $A$ is the product of $Y^d-\bar b$ over the $m/d$ distinct $d$-th powers $\bar b\in A^d$, which is $g(Y^d)$; expanding in powers of $Y$ shows that every coefficient with index not divisible by $d$ vanishes, i.e.\ $e_j(A)=0$ for $d\nmid j$.  Substituting $Y=\varphi(X)$ for the scale-$c$ map identifies the invariant stratum with the full stratum of the scale-$cd$ map $\varphi_d\circ\varphi$ of degree $cd$ \textup(for $\varphi=X^c$ this is literally $X^{cd}$, and for Chebyshev scales $T_d\circ T_c=T_{cd}$\textup); the number of pigeonholed slots and the subset count transform to exactly those of \cref{prop:graded-prefix-floor} at scale $cd$, cf.\ \cref{lem:v13-quot-pullback} for the same bijection at the locator level.  Since \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} optimizes over all dyadic scales, the invariant strata are already included.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[terminal-scale planting loses one step]\label{rem:v13f-planting}
Planting $\sigma<c$ extra agreement points inside one unused fiber \textup(the mechanism of \cref{thm:v13-planted}, run on top of the prefix floor\textup) raises the agreement by $\sigma$ at the cost of one additional pinned slot: at $c=2$, $\sigma=1$, the degree bound forces $w$ to increase by exactly one, multiplying the pigeonhole cost by $|\B|$.  A one-step decrease of $m$ changes the exact test by $\log_2|\B|+\log_2\frac{m+1}{N-m}\approx31.18$ bits, while the extra slot costs $\log_2|\B|\approx30.99$ bits; since the exact pass margins at all four optima of \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} are smaller than $30.99$ bits, the planted hybrid passes only after decreasing $m$ by at least one, for a net agreement change of $2(m-1)+1-2m=-1$.  So at the deployed sizes the plain floor at the terminal scale strictly dominates the planted hybrid, by exact arithmetic.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The frontier function and the closing conjecture}

\begin{definition}[entropy--subfield frontier function]\label{def:v13f-gstar}
For $\rho\in(0,1)$ and $\beta>0$, let
\[
        g^*(\rho,\beta):=\sup\{\,g\in(0,1-\rho]:\ \Hb(\rho+g)\ge\beta g\,\},
\]
the route ceiling of \cref{rem:entropy-frontier}.  For the two deployed base primes, $\beta_{\mathrm{KB}}=\log_2(2^{31}-2^{24}+1)=30.988685\ldots$ and $\beta_{\mathrm{M31}}=\log_2(2^{31}-1)=30.999999\ldots$, the values are
\[
\begin{array}{c|cccc}
\rho & 1/2 & 1/4 & 1/8 & 1/16\\ \hline
g^*(\rho,\beta_{\mathrm{KB}}) & 0.0321734 & 0.0274944 & 0.0192053 & 0.0123886\\
1-\rho-g^*(\rho,\beta_{\mathrm{KB}}) & 0.4678266 & 0.7225056 & 0.8557947 & 0.9251114\\ \hline
g^*(\rho,\beta_{\mathrm{M31}}) & 0.0321617 & 0.0274839 & 0.0191977 & 0.0123835\\
1-\rho-g^*(\rho,\beta_{\mathrm{M31}}) & 0.4678383 & 0.7225161 & 0.8558023 & 0.9251165
\end{array}
\]
\textup(seven-digit truncations of the exact solutions of $\Hb(\rho+g)=\beta g$\textup).  The terminal-scale certificates of \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} realize $g=0.0321684\ldots$ at $n=2^{21}$ on the KoalaBear MCA row, within $5.1\cdot10^{-6}$ of $g^*$; the same exact scan at $n=2^{22}$ and $n=2^{24}$ locates route optima within $2.7\cdot10^{-6}$ and $6.0\cdot10^{-7}$ of $g^*$ respectively.  The unsafe side of the sandwich is therefore saturated: within the graded-prefix route, larger blocklength buys less than $10^{-5}$ of additional radius, and by \cref{rem:entropy-frontier} no scale choice passes $g^*$ at any size.
\end{definition}

\begin{problem}[the frontier conjecture: exact challenge threshold]\label{prob:v13f-frontier}
Prove or refute: for every smooth multiplicative or circle row in the challenge envelope, with base field $\B$, $\beta=\log_2|\B|$, and target $\varepsilon^*$ as deployed,
\[
        \delta^*_C(\varepsilon^*)\ =\ 1-\rho-g^*(\rho,\beta)+o(1)
        \qquad(n\to\infty),
\]
for the MCA and the list challenge alike; equivalently, in exact finite form: the first safe agreement of the staircase of \cref{thm:v13-staircase} is
\[
        a_*\ =\ a_0+1,
\]
where $a_0$ is the terminal-scale route agreement of \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} \textup(at the deployed rows: $a_0=1116038$, so the conjectured adjacent pair is unsafe at agreement $1116038$ --- proved --- and safe at $1116039$ --- open\textup).  The inequality $\delta^*_C\le1-\rho-g+o(1)$ is proved for every $g<g^*$ by \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} and \cref{rem:entropy-frontier}; the conjecture is the matching safe side.
\end{problem}

\begin{fact}[provable floors for the conjecture window]\label{fact:v13f-floors}
The falsification window of \cref{prob:v13f-frontier} is bounded:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\roman*)}]
\item \textup{(list rows.)}  If $\varepsilon^*q\ge n+1$, then for every radius $\delta$ with $\ceil{(1-\delta)n}^2\ge kn+1$ the list challenge is safe; hence no witness of any kind can push the list unsafe edge below the Johnson radius, and
\[
        \delta^*_{\mathrm{list}}\in\bigl[\,1-\sqrt\rho,\ 1-\rho-g^*\,\bigr]+o(1)
\]
unconditionally \textup(at $\rho=\tfrac12$: $[0.292893,\,0.467827]$; at $\rho=\tfrac14$: $[0.5,\,0.722506]$\textup).
\item \textup{(MCA rows.)}  $\delta^*_{\mathrm{mca}}\ge\tfrac{1-\rho}3-o(1)$ self-contained by \cref{thm:deep-mca}, and $\ge\tfrac{1-\rho}2-o(1)$ with the import of \cref{cor:conditional-half}; so the MCA window is $[\tfrac{1-\rho}2,\,1-\rho-g^*]$ modulo the import.
\end{enumerate}
\end{fact}

\begin{proof}
\textup{(i)}  Put $s=\ceil{(1-\delta)n}$.  If $s^2\ge kn+1$, then $s^2-(k-1)n\ge n+1$, and \cref{thm:johnson-list} \textup(equivalently \cref{thm:v13-johnson-list} with $\kappa=k-1$\textup) bounds every list by
\[
        L\le\frac{n(s-k+1)}{s^2-(k-1)n}\le\frac{n\cdot n}{n+1}<n+1\le\varepsilon^*q ,
\]
so every radius below the Johnson radius is safe for the list challenge at the stated envelope condition, which the deployed rows satisfy.  The right endpoint is \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} with \cref{rem:entropy-frontier}.  \textup{(ii)} is the quoted pair of safe-side theorems.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Reduction of the conjecture to two named inputs}

\begin{proposition}[conditional closing theorem]\label{prop:v13f-closing}
Fix a deployed row and its target, and let $a_0$ be its terminal-scale route agreement from \cref{prop:v13f-terminal}.  Suppose the following two inputs are proved for every received line of the row, at every agreement $a$ with $a_0<a<n-\floor{(n-k)/3}$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(Q)},leftmargin=2.6em]
\item the quotient-profile upper ledger of \cref{thm:conditional-mca}\textup{(i)} evaluates, at agreement $a$, to a per-line numerator at most the equidistribution size of the prefix fibers, up to a polynomial factor:
\[
        B_Q^{\mathrm{all}}(a)\ \le\ \mathrm{poly}(n)\cdot\max_{c\mid n}\ \frac{\binom{n/c}{\ceil{a/c}}}{|\B|^{\,\ceil{a/c}-\ceil{k/c}}}\ ,
\]
\end{enumerate}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(A)},leftmargin=2.6em]
\item the aperiodic band bound of \cref{prob:band} holds with its polynomial $P$.
\end{enumerate}
Then, with the extension accounting supplied by \cref{thm:weil-lines} and the line-family identification of \cref{thm:conditional-mca}\textup{(iv)}, the hypotheses of \cref{thm:conditional-mca} are met with an upper numerator satisfying $B_{\mathrm{mca}}(a_0+1)\le2^{-128}q_{\mathrm{line}}$, while \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} supplies $B_{\mathrm{mca}}(a_0)>2^{-128}q_{\mathrm{line}}$.  By \cref{prop:onestep} \textup(equivalently \cref{thm:v13-staircase,thm:v13-corridor}\textup) the adjacent pair pins the threshold exactly:
\[
        a_*=a_0+1,
        \qquad
        \delta^*_C(2^{-128})=1-\frac{a_0}{n},
\]
i.e.\ \cref{prob:v13f-frontier} holds for the row.  In particular, the frontier conjecture is implied by \textup{(Q)} and \textup{(A)}, and \textup{(Q)} at linear gap is precisely the per-fiber collision problem isolated in \cite{Cho26b}: fibers of the prefix maps $A\mapsto(e_1(A),\ldots,e_w(A))$ on $m$-subsets of the quotient must not exceed their equidistribution size $\binom Nm|\B|^{-w}$ by more than a polynomial factor.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
At agreement $a>a_0$, the frontier inequality fails by definition of the route optimum: $\Hb(a/n)<\beta(a-k)/n+o(1)$, exactly as quantified by the finite-size scan of \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} \textup(the certificate fails at $a_0+1$ at every scale, with the printed margins\textup).  Under \textup{(Q)}, the per-line quotient numerator at agreement $a_0+1$ is therefore at most $\mathrm{poly}(n)\cdot2^{-\Omega(1)}\cdot2^{\log_2 q-128}$ --- the same exact comparison that fails the certificate, read as an upper bound --- and under \textup{(A)} the aperiodic contribution is $P(n)$; the tangent stratum contributes at most $n-a+1$ per line by \cref{thm:deep-mca} where $3(n-a)\le n-k$, and above that agreement the deep bound applies directly.  Adding the strata as in \cref{thm:conditional-mca} gives $B_{\mathrm{mca}}(a_0+1)\le2^{-128}q_{\mathrm{line}}$ once $n$ exceeds the explicit polynomial thresholds; the deployed sizes do by the printed margins.  The lower half of the adjacent pair is \cref{prop:v13f-terminal}.  \Cref{prop:onestep} then converts the pair into the exact threshold statement, and the identification of \textup{(Q)} with the per-fiber collision problem is the definition of the quotient ledger at complete-fiber supports: each quotient-periodic listed codeword at scale $c$ is a prefix-fiber member, so the per-line ledger is the fiber size of the prefix map at the pinned value, cf.\ \cref{lem:quotient-remainder-prefix}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{First proved cases of input \textup{(Q)}: bounded-prefix equidistribution}

The deployed terminal scale operates in the Weil range: the quotient $\mu_N$, $N=2^{20}$, is a multiplicative subgroup of $\F_p^\times$ of order \emph{exceeding} $\sqrt p\approx46160$.  This makes the base cases of the per-fiber collision problem provable today, with enormous margins.

\begin{theorem}[bounded-prefix equidistribution over large subgroups]\label{thm:v13f-collision-base}
Let $p$ be prime, $H\le\F_p^\times$ of order $N$, $d=(p-1)/N$, and $S=\alpha H$ a coset.  Let $1\le w<N$ with $wd<p$, let $m\le N$, and for $\vec z\in\F_p^w$ put
\[
        \mathcal N_w(m,\vec z)\ :=\ \#\bigl\{A\subseteq S:\ |A|=m,\ \bigl(e_1(A),\ldots,e_w(A)\bigr)=\vec z\,\bigr\}.
\]
Then, with $B:=\ceil{\sqrt p}$, for every $\vec z$,
\[
        \Bigl|\,\mathcal N_w(m,\vec z)-\frac1{p^w}\binom Nm\,\Bigr|
        \ \le\ p^{w/2}\binom{wB+m-1}{m},
\]
and for $w=1$ the factor $p^{1/2}$ may be dropped.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fourier inversion on $\F_p^w$ gives $\mathcal N_w(m,\vec z)=p^{-w}\sum_{\vec t}e_p(-\vec t\cdot\vec z)E_m(\vec t)$ with
$E_m(\vec t):=\sum_{|A|=m}e_p\bigl(\sum_jt_je_j(A)\bigr)$; the term $\vec t=0$ contributes $\binom Nm p^{-w}$, so it suffices to bound $|E_m(\vec t)|$ for $\vec t\ne0$ by the displayed error.

Fix $\vec t\ne0$ and let $j^*=\max\{j:t_j\ne0\}$.  By the Newton identities \textup(divisions by $j\le w<p$ are legitimate\textup), each $e_j$ is a polynomial in the power sums $p_1,\ldots,p_j$, with $e_j=(-1)^{j-1}p_j/j+R_j(p_1,\ldots,p_{j-1})$; hence $\sum_jt_je_j(A)=Q(P(A))$ for a polynomial $Q$ on $\F_p^{j^*}$, where $P(A)=(p_1(A),\ldots,p_{j^*}(A))$ and
\[
        \partial Q/\partial v_{j^*}=(-1)^{j^*-1}t_{j^*}/j^*\ \ne\ 0
        \quad\text{is constant.}
\]
Writing $M(\vec v)=\#\{|A|=m:P(A)=\vec v\}$ and inverting again,
\[
        E_m(\vec t)\ =\ p^{-j^*}\sum_{\vec s\in\F_p^{j^*}}\widehat Q(\vec s)\,G(\vec s),
        \qquad
        \widehat Q(\vec s)=\sum_{\vec v}e_p\bigl(Q(\vec v)-\vec s\cdot\vec v\bigr),
        \quad
        G(\vec s)=\sum_{|A|=m}e_p\bigl(\vec s\cdot P(A)\bigr).
\]
The term $\vec s=0$ vanishes: since $Q$ is linear in $v_{j^*}$ with constant nonzero coefficient, the inner sum over $v_{j^*}$ in $\widehat Q(0)$ is a complete nontrivial character sum, so $\widehat Q(0)=0$.

For $\vec s\ne0$, $G(\vec s)=e_m\bigl((\varepsilon_a)_{a\in S}\bigr)$ with $\varepsilon_a=e_p(g_s(a))$, $g_s(x)=\sum_{i\le j^*}s_ix^i$ of degree $i^*\le w$; its power sums are $\pi_j:=\sum_{a\in S}e_p(jg_s(a))$, $1\le j\le m$.  Since $\deg g_s\le w<N$ and $g_s$ is nonzero of positive degree, $g_s$ is nonconstant on the $N$-point coset $S$, and $j\not\equiv0\pmod p$ because $j\le m\le N<p$.  Parametrizing $S=\{\alpha y^d:y\in\F_p^\times\}$ with each point covered $d$ times,
\[
        \pi_j=\frac1d\Bigl[\sum_{y\in\F_p}e_p\bigl(F(y)\bigr)-e_p(F(0))\Bigr],
        \qquad F(y)=jg_s(\alpha y^d),\ \deg F=i^*d\le wd<p,
\]
and the Weil bound for additive character sums \textup(applicable since $1\le\deg F<p$, so $\gcd(\deg F,p)=1$\textup) gives $|\pi_j|\le\frac1d\bigl[(i^*d-1)\sqrt p+1\bigr]\le w\sqrt p\le wB$.

Newton domination converts the power-sum bound into an elementary-symmetric bound: the recursion $me_m=\sum_{i=1}^m(-1)^{i-1}e_{m-i}\pi_i$ is majorized coefficientwise by $mc_m=wB\sum_{i=1}^mc_{m-i}$, whose solution is $c_m=[u^m](1-u)^{-wB}=\binom{wB+m-1}m$; hence $|G(\vec s)|\le\binom{wB+m-1}m$ for every $\vec s\ne0$.  Finally, by Cauchy--Schwarz and Parseval, $\sum_{\vec s}|\widehat Q(\vec s)|\le p^{j^*/2}\bigl(\sum_{\vec s}|\widehat Q(\vec s)|^2\bigr)^{1/2}=p^{j^*/2}\cdot p^{j^*}$, so
\[
        |E_m(\vec t)|\ \le\ p^{-j^*}\cdot p^{3j^*/2}\cdot\binom{wB+m-1}m\ =\ p^{j^*/2}\binom{wB+m-1}m\ \le\ p^{w/2}\binom{wB+m-1}m .
\]
For $w=1$ no linearization is needed: $E_m(t)=e_m\bigl((e_p(ta))_{a\in S}\bigr)$ directly, with $|\pi_j|\le\sqrt p$ by the same parametrization at degree $d$, giving $|E_m(t)|\le\binom{B+m-1}m$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[the first ten rungs of the prefix staircase are exact]\label{cor:v13f-Q-base}
At the deployed terminal scale of \cref{prop:v13f-terminal} \textup(KoalaBear rows: $N=2^{20}$, $d=2032$, $m=558019$ resp.\ $558022$\textup), \cref{thm:v13f-collision-base} is nonvacuous for exactly $w\le10$: the fiber deviation is smaller than the equidistribution size $\binom Nm p^{-w}$ by
\[
        +810165,\ +662081,\ +544127,\ +444385,\ +357328,\ +279781,\ +209702,\ +145679,\ +86685,\ +31947
\]
bits at $w=1,\ldots,10$, and the estimate fails at $w=11$; at depth $w=1$ it is nonvacuous at every scale $c\le16$.  Consequently:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textup{(\alph*)}]
\item every prefix fiber of depth $w\le10$ has size $\binom Nm p^{-w}\bigl(1+\varepsilon_w\bigr)$ with $|\varepsilon_1|\le2^{-810165}$ and analogously through $w=10$, so the pigeonhole floors of \cref{prop:graded-prefix-floor} are \emph{exact} at these depths, and the corresponding rungs of the unsafe staircase are two-sidedly priced --- the first exactly priced strata of the programme;
\item input \textup{(Q)} of \cref{prop:v13f-closing} is \emph{proved}, with no polynomial factor needed, for all quotient-periodic strata of prefix depth $j^*\le10$, i.e.\ at terminal-scale agreements $a\le k+2+2w$, $w\le10$;
\item the identity steps and both character-sum bounds were verified exhaustively over $\F_{257}$ with the order-$16$ subgroup, including the exact vanishing $\widehat Q(0)=0$ and the joint $(e_1,e_2)$-fiber census.
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[what a full proof of \textup{(Q)} now needs]\label{rem:v13f-collision-gap}
The proof of \cref{thm:v13f-collision-base} pays one Weil cost $\sqrt p$ \emph{per power sum}, i.e.\ $wB$ in the domination step, and this is exactly what caps the reach at $w=10$: the band frontier sits at $w\approx33731$, where $\binom{wB+m-1}m$ has long overtaken $\binom Nm$.  The sharp remaining statement for \textup{(Q)} is therefore square-root cancellation in the \emph{joint} sum,
\[
        \bigl|E_m(\vec t)\bigr|=\Bigl|\sum_{|A|=m}e_p\Bigl(\sum_{j\le w}t_je_j(A)\Bigr)\Bigr|
        \ \le\ \binom Nm\cdot p^{-\kappa w}\qquad(\vec t\ne0)
\]
for some fixed $\kappa>0$ uniformly in $w$ --- a Deligne-range estimate on the symmetric-power side, replacing the termwise Newton domination.  Any such bound closes \textup{(Q)} at all depths at once, and \cref{prob:v13f-frontier} then reduces to input \textup{(A)} alone.  For the circle rows the same argument runs through the Chebyshev parametrization $x=(u+u^{-1})/2$ on the twin coset, with Laurent--Weil bounds and doubled degree constants; we record the multiplicative case as the theorem and leave the routine circle transcription to the row packets.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[evidence, and the falsification target]\label{rem:v13f-evidence}
Three structural facts support the frontier conjecture.  \textup{(a)}  Over \emph{random} evaluation domains, Reed--Solomon codes achieve list-decoding capacity with bounded list sizes \textup(Brakensiek--Gopi--Makam; Guo--Zhang; Alrabiah--Guruswami--Tamo\textup), so any failure below capacity must exploit the structure of the domain; on smooth domains the structural witness inventory --- quotient locators, graded prefixes, planted cores --- is exactly what the route already optimizes, and \cref{lem:v13f-scale-absorption} with \cref{rem:v13f-planting} shows this inventory is closed under its known free operations.  \textup{(b)}  The additive analogues that break Johnson-type barriers for Reed--Solomon codes \textup(subspace-polynomial received words in the style of Ben-Sasson--Kopparty--Radhakrishnan\textup) require $\F_2$-linear evaluation sets, unavailable inside odd-characteristic multiplicative cosets and circle domains.  \textup{(c)}  The frontier depends on the base field only through $\beta=\log_2|\B|$, matching the subfield-confinement phenomenon of \cref{sec:subfield}: larger base fields provably narrow the certifiable band, and the conjecture predicts the true threshold narrows with it.  The falsification target is correspondingly sharp: a witness family whose list exponent at gap $g$ exceeds $\tfrac nc\Hb(\rho+g)-\beta g\tfrac nc$ for some $g>g^*$ at some scale, which by \cref{prop:v13f-closing} must be either aperiodic in the sense of \cref{def:periodicity-scale} or exhibit super-equidistribution concentration of prefix fibers.  Both possibilities are exactly the two named inputs, so the conjecture cannot fail silently: any counterexample resolves \textup{(Q)} or \textup{(A)} in the negative, and any proof of \textup{(Q)} and \textup{(A)} closes the band.
\end{remark}

\end{remark}
